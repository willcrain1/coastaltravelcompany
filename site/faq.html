Pricing & Collections

Each collection is a defined project engagement with a base investment tailored to scope and scale. Pricing reflects travel, shoot days, editing depth, and deliverable volume — not an hourly rate.

The Editorial Stay is our most comprehensive offering: multi-day, full-property coverage. The Fashioned Weekend is a focused two-day engagement built for lifestyle and social content. The Signature Story is our campaign-level collection for brands that want a complete visual identity.

Exact investment is provided after an initial conversation — properties vary too much in scope for a single public price point to be meaningful. Contact us to receive a proposal tailored to your property.

Every collection includes pre-shoot consultation, professional editing, a private client gallery, and a commercial-use license for the delivered images. Specific deliverables depend on the collection:

  • The Editorial Stay — full-property still photography across all key spaces, delivered as a complete campaign library
  • The Fashioned Weekend — lifestyle photography with a fashion-forward perspective, plus short-form video content optimized for social platforms
  • The Signature Story — everything in the above collections plus a hero film, complete creative direction, and a brand-defining visual library

Add-ons such as rush delivery, extended licensing, additional shoot days, and 3D walkthroughs are available on any collection. Visit the Services page for full details.

Yes. Rush delivery is available as a paid add-on if you need final images sooner than the standard turnaround window. This is discussed during the proposal stage and confirmed before the shoot so we can plan editing time accordingly.

Licensing & Usage Rights

All collections include a commercial license for digital use — website, social media, digital advertising, and email marketing — for the duration specified in your contract (typically three years, renewable).

Print use (brochures, signage, magazines) and extended exclusivity are available as add-ons. Sub-licensing to third parties (e.g. a booking platform or PR agency) requires a separate extended license.

For full details on what each tier covers, see our Licensing page.

Yes. Coastal Travel Company retains copyright in all images. Your license grants you the right to use the photos within the agreed scope — it does not transfer ownership of the copyright. This is standard practice for professional photography and protects both parties.

If you need a work-for-hire arrangement where copyright transfers to you, this can be discussed and is quoted separately.

Travel & Logistics

We're based in Palm Beach, Florida, and available for travel worldwide. Travel fees apply for properties outside the Palm Beach metro area and cover flights, accommodation, ground transport, and per diem — billed at cost. These are itemized in your proposal so there are no surprises.

For multi-day shoots at a destination property, we typically arrive the day before the shoot begins to do a walkthrough and plan the shot sequence with your team.

We recommend a minimum of six to eight weeks lead time — this allows time for proposal, contract, pre-shoot consultation, and travel logistics. For peak seasons (late autumn and winter in Florida, summer in European destinations) or multi-week campaigns, three to four months is advisable.

Check the availability calendar on our Contact page for open dates, or reach out directly to discuss your timeline.

After booking, we'll send a pre-shoot questionnaire to gather property details, must-have shots, access contacts, and any restrictions. Key things that make a shoot run smoothly:

  • Rooms staged and cleaned before our arrival (including removing personal items, power cables, and toiletries)
  • A dedicated property contact available on shoot days for access and quick decisions
  • Pool and outdoor areas clear of guests during scheduled exterior slots
  • Lighting conditions noted — we'll plan indoor versus outdoor sequences around the light

We'll walk through everything in a pre-shoot call so your team knows exactly what to expect.

The Shoot

Shoot days typically begin at sunrise or shortly after — early light is the most flattering for interiors and outdoor spaces. We work through a pre-agreed shot list but stay flexible to respond to conditions and spontaneous moments.

You don't need to be on-site the entire time, but a point of contact who can quickly open rooms or clear spaces makes a significant difference. We handle all equipment, styling direction, and scene composition — your role is simply to facilitate access.

For multi-day collections, we'll debrief at the end of each day to review coverage and adjust the next day's plan if needed.

Standard turnaround is two to four weeks from the final shoot day, depending on collection volume. You'll receive a private, password-protected client gallery with full-resolution downloads. Rush delivery within one week is available as an add-on.

A selection of hero images may be delivered sooner as a priority batch if you need something for an announcement or campaign launch.

Final images are delivered via a private client gallery accessible from your account on this website. You can preview every image, download individual files or the full set, and share access with your marketing team — all from one URL. The gallery remains accessible for the duration of your license period.

Booking

The process is straightforward:

  • 1. Inquire — submit the contact form with your property name, preferred dates, and the collection you're interested in
  • 2. Proposal — we'll send a tailored proposal within 48 hours for you to review
  • 3. Contract & deposit — sign digitally and pay a 50% retainer to secure your shoot dates
  • 4. Pre-shoot — complete the questionnaire and join a brief call to align on the shot plan
  • 5. Shoot — we arrive, capture, and deliver

The full balance is due upon gallery delivery.

Cancellations made more than 30 days before the shoot date are eligible for a full refund of the retainer, minus a non-refundable booking fee. Cancellations within 30 days forfeit the retainer. Rescheduling is accommodated when possible subject to availability — no rescheduling fee if notice is given more than 14 days out.

Full cancellation terms are detailed in your contract.
